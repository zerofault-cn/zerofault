1. Port Range Forwarding
Opens a port or a range of ports to a single IP address on a network.

2. Port Triggering
Similar to Port Forwarding, except: a) an outgoing trigger port is required before incoming ports are forwarded, and, b) outgoing and incoming ports are applicable to all IP addresses on the same network.

3. UPnP Forwarding
same as Port Forwaring but the port or port range can be configured by any UPnP enabled application, e.g., MSN Messenger or Windows Live Messenger.
